The Lighthouse Keeper

Ronda Armitage and David Armitage

Industrious Mr Grinling, his clever wife, Mrs Grinling, and masterful cat, Hamish, tend the lighthouse, get into one scrape after another, but always find a solution.

  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Rescue by Ronda Armitage; David Armitage (Illustrator). Mr Grinling maroons himself and his wife, leading to a change of responsibilities. (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Lunch by Ronda Armitage and David Armitage. When gulls steal Mr Ginling's lunch each day, he and Mrs Grinling try many defenses before they hit on a solution. (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Picnic by Ronda Armitage; David Armitage (Illustrator). Mr Grinling eats a bit too much at the picnic. (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Tea by Ronda Armitage and David Armitage. Mr Grinling perseveres at a new sport. (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Cat by Ronda Armitage and David Armitage. Hamish the cat does not want to be put on a diet, and an adventure ensues. (ages 4 to 6) (out-of-print, see sources.)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Catastrophe by Ronda Armitage; David Armitage (Illustrator). (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Breakfast by Ronda Armitage; David Armitage (Illustrator). Mr and Mrs Grinling want to be pirates, and their wish appears to have come at bit more true than they bargained for. (ages 4 to 6)
  • The Lighthouse Keeper's Christmas by Ronda Armitage and David Armitage. Mr Grinling is stranded on Christmas eve and fears for the worst -- but all comes right in the end. (ages 4 to 6)
